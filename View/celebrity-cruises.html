The Celebrity Cruises fleet is divided into four ship classes that all have something different to offer. All ships are the epitome of modern luxury, providing superior guest service and unmissable onboard activities.

The Edge Class ships are the newest to join the fleet and are the most modern in design with features such as the remarkable Magic Carpet dining experience, which is a suspended, cantilevered platform on the ship's starboard side. The Grand Plaza is a glamorous gathering space that includes several restaurants and bars. The Edge Class ships also feature the newly designed Infinite Veranda Staterooms where, with the touch of a button, your entire living space becomes the veranda, letting you walk right out to the water's edge.

There are four Edge Class ships within the Celebrity Cruises fleet for you to explore.

If you enjoy a more intimate atmosphere on board, with a couple of hundred fewer passengers, you might feel more at ease onboard one of Celebrity's Millennium Class vessels. There are a total of four cruise ships in this class for you to explore the world with.

The Solstice Class is made up of five ships and is a larger design than the Millennium Class thus holding a greater number of passengers on board. Rest assured there'll be no queues or overcrowding, though, thanks to excellent passenger flow. Solstice-Class vessels also include innovative facilities such as the top-of-the-ship Lawn Club, featuring real grass. The Xpedition-Class ships are small in design and only hold up to 100 guests. There are three ships in this class and are designed specifically to explore the Galapagos Islands.

Because the Galapagos Islands have very strict regulations regarding cruise ship operations in the archipelagos, Celebrity Cruises is one of the few cruise lines with permission to offer sailings in the islands.
